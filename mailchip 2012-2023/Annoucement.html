Announcing Chrome Web Lab, a year-long interactive exhibition by Google Creative Lab and Tellart


July 19, 2012

Dear friends, family, clients and colleagues:

 
Tellart is excited to announce its newest project: the Google Chrome Web Lab exhibition at the Science Museum in London– and around the world. You are invited to visit it at www.chromeweblab.com on its public beta launch today, July 19, 2012.

Web Lab is a groundbreaking, year-long exhibition, featuring a series of interactive Chrome Experiments that bring the extraordinary workings of the internet to life. Google Creative Lab and Tellart created the user experience design for the Web Lab, and engineered and produced hardware and software for the physical exhibition.

Visitors to the Science Museum and online visitors to www.chromeweblab.com will be able to play with five unique experiments. Museum visitors and online visitors alike can compose together on giant musical instruments, have their faces drawn in sand by robot arms, teleport to off-limits locations through 360° streaming video, interact with live data paths on a world map, and more.  Visitors are each given a unique avatar that allows them to collect the artifacts of their interactions and to be virtually represented in an expansive, mixed-reality visualization space within the exhibit.  

We hope you’ll take a moment to visit www.chromeweblab.com and experience it for yourself.  If you’re in London, we encourage you to check it out in person:

The Science Museum
Exhibition Rd
South Kensington
London SW7 2DD
July 19, 2012 - Summer 2013

Thanks to all who participate, and we can’t wait to see your creations!


Sincerely,
All of us at Tellart
